Honeybee Colony Inventory on Farms across the Contiguous United States

This interactive map illustrates the inventory of honeybee colonies on farms across the contiguous United States from 1987 to 2017. Honeybees, along with other bee species, provide the critical ecosystem service of pollination for over 90 major food crops, and are responsible for 3 out of every 4 bites of food you consume. Due to the global decline of wild honeybee populations, farms are increasingly reliant on purchased or rented honeybee colonies to pollinate their crops.Honeybee decline poses major threats to food security and has serious economic and environmental implications. In response to global population growth, agricultural intensification will increase, which will lead to more pesticide use, habitat degradation, and biodiversity loss.
